About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Environmental Planning at UC Berkeley, where I research the intersection of GeoAI, natural hazards, and urban resilience. My work integrates computer vision, machine learning, and geospatial modeling to develop solutions for complex socio-environmental systems. My background includes both civil engineering and environmental data science. I am passionate about building models and tools that inform planning decisions for communities facing wildfire, debris flow, and climate risks. I actively collaborate with local governments, NGOs, and interdisciplinary researchers to translate data science into actionable insights for real-world impact.

Outside of academia, I enjoy hiking, photography, and contributing to open-source geospatial projects.
